LEAFY

House Plant Online Shop Adaptive Website

Project Overview

Designing a website for a local greenhouse was one of the student assignment we had in UX-Land school. In a team of four, we were assigned to design an adaptive website for both mobile and desktop devices focusing on User Interface aspect.


We defined Leafy as an online store, in which using Plant Finder can suggest the right plant based on the users’ need.


As this is a school concept project, we defined a hypothetical stakeholder at the beginning and consider his requirements and limitations throughout the process.

Duration

4 Weeks

Team

Group of 4

Role

UX/UI Designer

Tools

Target User

The website is designed to serve both plant experts and those who are not experienced.

Challenges

The challenge was to showcase the plants as a living beings rather than objects in a market place.

Showing real images indicating the actual size/scale of the plant, instructions on how to maintain the same and being able to find the right plant based on the user’s need.

By analyzing the information we obtained through surveys and interviews, we realized the problem most users have when buying plants online, is lack of information to choose, maintain or to keep the plant healthy.

Our Approach

  • Providing sufficient information for each plant on how to maintain one.

  • Personalize guide to ease purchasing of a plant based on the user’s need.

  • Ensuring plant good health condition by showing reviews and testimonials of previous users.

  • Showing process of preparation, packaging and delivery of plants.

Wbsites Analyzed

Define

Persona

Lily Moss | 27 Years Old | Accountant

Demography: Lily is 27 years old, lives in London, UK. She is single, and has a middle-income-level.

Lily’s story: Since pandemic, Lily is mostly working from home and only visits the office once a week. As she spends most of her time at home, she decided to buy some house plants to enhance the surrounding.

Lily’s Challenge: Lily doesn't have enough information about house plants in general. She also has a pet, for which she needs to make sure the plant she buys is pet friendly. She has difficulties trusting the information and images provided on some platforms.

Lily’s Expectations: Lily needs to find a right plant in a simple way. She needs to have easy access to information about her plant in general and how to maintain it. She needs to find a platform offering right and honest information, true images ensuring the health and safely delivery of the plants.

Needs:

  • Improve productivity and concentration
  • Improve indoor air quality and ambiance
  • Improve mental and physical health

Challenges:

  • To get consultation for getting suitable plant
  • Getting honest information about plants’ health status
  • Safe delivery

Expectations:

  • Easy access to information about plants
  • Healthy plant
  • Guaranteed Safe delivery of plant
  • Honest images and related input
  • To be able to get accessories and plant supplements

A/B testing

This A/B test was run among 8 participants. We evaluated the user satisfaction and functionality of two different designs for a plant search questionnaire.

The results showed that (Sample A) was preferred the most among participants in terms of functionality and appearance compared to (Sample B). Hence we proceeded accordingly.

What did I learn?

  • Making a good balance of users' needs and stakeholders' marketing strategy in mind while designing.

  • Effective communication with team members and lead challenging situations.

  • How to design a website from complete scratch.

  • My first student project exposed me to the importance of understanding users' needs at every stage of the design process. The best way to gain this overview is by keeping track of all research findings and referring to them continually.

  • It was interesting to discover that I enjoyed interaction design the most, and I'll continue to develop this skill in the next projects.

What can we do next?
