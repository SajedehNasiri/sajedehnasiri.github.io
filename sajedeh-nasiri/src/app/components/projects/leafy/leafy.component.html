LEAFY

House Plant Online Shop Adaptive Website

Project Overview

Designing a website for a local greenhouse was one of the student assignment we had in UX-Land school. In a team of four, we were assigned to design an adaptive website for both mobile and desktop devices focusing on User Interface aspect.


We defined Leafy as an online store, in which using Plant Finder can suggest the right plant based on the users’ need.


We have also created an opportunity for the user to try different pots after selecting a plant.

Role

UX/UI Designer

Team

8 Weeks

Tools

Figma, Photoshop, Zoom, Google Form

Business Need

Creating an e-commerce website with an appealing user experience to sell plants online

Target User

The website is designed to serve both plant experts and those who are not experienced.

Our Approach

  • Float surveys to understand why users avoid buying plants online

  • Interviewed potential users to understand their concerns and needs

  • Studied the process of shopping plants in person so that we can simulate similar situation.

Solution Provided

The idea of “Plant Finder” was born when trying to create a similar experience with in-person shopping. Using this platform, based on the data collected from a user through a series of questions, the perfect match is proposed.

We also noticed that our users are interested in buying a suitable pot while buying a plant. Therefore, we designed a feature that allows them to choose their favorite pot along with the plant during the purchase process.

Competitive Analysis

During our analysis, we have come to the realization that there are numerous ways to categorize plants, depending on various factors such as the user's specific needs, geographical location, and business value. Consequently, we have developed an Information Architecture that serves to both plant experts and those with little or no experience, ensuring that everyone can benefit from it.

Challenges and Solutions

Upon thorough analysis of all our research data, we gained a comprehensive understanding of the challenges associated with our project and attempted to address them by incorporating a solution into our design.

Challenges

Solution

Showing plants as a living beings rather than objects in a market place

  • Provided all the information about each plant like an ID card where the scientific name, all characteristics, size, maintenance and all other information is shown.

Showing true images communicating the size/scale

  • Provided images of a person holding the plant could indicate the sense of scale.

  • Indicating the dimension of each plant.

Providing instructions on how to maintain a plant.

  • General info was shared as iconography.

  • Detailed info was showed as a video .

Trusting the health of the plants shown.

  • Provided guarantee on the health of the plant .

  • showing user reviews about each plant.

Trusting the packaging and safely delivering the plants.

  • Showing all the process of preparation and packaging through gif.

  • showing user testimonial reviews about the same.

Buying the right pot for each plant.

  • For each plant selected, a user can select suitable pots and see the result.

Being to find the right plant based on the user’s need; like a one to one consultation.

  • Plant Finder Task was designed which suggest a suitable plant by percentage.

  • Using icons to present plant’s info within a glance

Usability Test

We tested the task of buying a plant with a pot with 6 people. Through Usability testing when the task was given, we observed that the users did not notice the information that we had placed in the scroll and they lost a lot of important data. As a result, we took out the data from the scrollable frame so that all the necessary information can be seen with just one scroll.

A/B testing

This A/B test was run among 8 participants. We evaluated the user satisfaction and functionality of two different designs for a plant search questionnaire.

The results showed that (Sample A) was preferred the most among participants in terms of functionality and appearance compared to (Sample B). Hence we proceeded accordingly.

What did I learn?

  • Making a good balance of users' needs and stakeholders' marketing strategy in mind while designing.

  • Effective communication with team members and lead challenging situations.

  • How to design a website from complete scratch.

  • My first student project exposed me to the importance of understanding users' needs at every stage of the design process. The best way to gain this overview is by keeping track of all research findings and referring to them continually.
